What is 2+2?

The mathematical expression "2+2" is a simple addition problem that asks what the sum of the numbers 2 and 2 is. In other words, it is asking what number results when you add the quantity 2 to the quantity 2.

To solve this problem, we can use our knowledge of basic arithmetic. Addition is one of the fundamental mathematical operations, where we combine two or more numbers to get a new, larger number.

When we add 2 and 2 together, the result is 4. This is because:

  1. 2 represents a quantity of 2 units.
  2. 2 also represents a quantity of 2 units.
  3. When we add these two quantities together, we get a total of 4 units.
  4. Therefore, the answer to the expression "2+2" is 4.

In summary, 2+2=4 because when you combine two quantities of 2, the total amount is 4. This is a basic arithmetic fact that is fundamental to mathematics and can be easily verified through simple counting or using visual aids like blocks or fingers.
